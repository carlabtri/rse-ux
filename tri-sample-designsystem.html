Research domains

Five fronts, one mission

Each domain tackles a distinct facet of making the world safer and more sustainable — and they reinforce each other constantly.

Safety-first Scalable Inclusive

Automated Driving Advanced Development

We are developing automated driving technology that is safe, scalable, and inclusive — building systems that can handle complex, real-world environments and serve all people, not just those in ideal conditions.

  • Fleet-scale real-world testing across diverse road conditions
  • Behavior prediction and decision-making under uncertainty
  • Accessible mobility for older adults and people with disabilities
Learn more
Zero-emissions Materials AI Batteries

Energy & Materials

Accelerating the discovery of new materials for batteries, catalysts, and structural components using AI-guided simulation and high-throughput experimentation — targeting a zero-emissions future.

  • AI-accelerated molecular and materials simulation
  • Next-generation solid-state battery research
  • Hydrogen fuel-cell catalyst development
Learn more
Foundation models Safety Behavior

Human-Centered AI

Building AI systems that augment and amplify human capabilities — not replace them. We focus on prediction, planning, and decision-making grounded in how humans actually behave.

  • Human behavior modeling and intent prediction
  • Foundation models for scene understanding at scale
  • Safe and interpretable AI decision-making
Learn more
Driver experience Human factors Safety

Human Interactive Driving

Enhancing the relationship between driver and vehicle — creating intelligent systems that understand, support, and amplify the human driving experience rather than replacing it.

  • Driver state sensing and cognitive load modeling
  • Adaptive guardian systems that intervene only when needed
  • Personalized driving experience through real-time modeling
Learn more
Manipulation Learning Amplification

Robotics

Building robots that amplify human capabilities — systems that learn from human demonstration, collaborate naturally, and handle the physical tasks that are difficult, dangerous, or inaccessible to people.

  • Dexterous manipulation and general-purpose robot learning
  • Human-robot collaboration and task transfer
  • Assistive robotics for home and healthcare settings

Our approach

How we work

TRI sits at the intersection of fundamental research and applied engineering — we focus on problems where the gap between today's capabilities and what the world needs is largest.

Research first
Published in top venues with open collaboration
Applied impact
Technology transferred to Toyota vehicles worldwide
TRI combines the resources and long-term commitment of Toyota with the culture of academic research — giving us the freedom to tackle problems with 10-year time horizons while still delivering technology that reaches millions of people.
We partner with over 40 universities globally through multi-year funded collaborations, visiting researcher programs, and joint publications. Partners publish freely and retain IP generated at their institutions.
